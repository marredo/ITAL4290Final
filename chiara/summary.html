A Chiara - Summary

A Chiara, directed by Jonas Carpignano, follows the story of Chiara Guerrasio, a sixteen-year-old girl from the Calabrian town of Gioia Tauro. One night after her family is celebrating her sister's birthday a car explodes on her home's street and her father vanishes. Her family seems unconcerned with the sudden disappearance and Chiara begins investigating on her own and gradually discovers that her family is deeply involved in the Ndrangheta, the Calabrian mafia.

Her search leads her into a hidden world of criminal networks, clandestine safehouses, and unspoken family loyalties. As she investigates, the loving image she had of her father unravels, making her confront the gap between the life she believed she was living and the reality surrounding her. Her family, attempting to protect her from both danger and knowledge, refuses to answer her questions, making her more and more suspicious.

Social services determine Chiara's home and familial environment to be unsafe and she is sent to a foster home far away from all she has known. The film ends with her beginning athletic training as she turns eighteen an open-ended image suggesting both loss and the possibility of a new life.
